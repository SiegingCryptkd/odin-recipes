Pho

What is Pho?

Pho is a popular and my personal favorite Vietnamese soup dish (yes... you can call me basic) that consists of bone broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat. It can be made from any type of bone (beef, pork, chicken etc) but beef is the most common version. Pho has a clean yet savory, delicate taste and has the potential to give you an otherworldly experience. Everytime I eat pho, it brings me back to childhood memories of my mom cooking preparing it. The rich smell of all the different spices would always make my stomach rumble from hunger! Let's get to prepping the ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 4lb of beef soup bones (shank and knee)
  • 1 (8oz) pack of dry Rice noodles
  • Beef sirloin
  • 2 1/2 tbl spoon of Fish sauce
  • 2 unpeeled Onions, 1 cut in half, 1 sliced into thin pieces
  • 4 quarts of water

    • Spices and Seasonings

    • 5 slices of fresh Ginger
    • 2 pods of Star anise
    • 1 tbl spoon of Salt

    Garnishes

    • 1/2 cup of chopped Cilantro
    • 1 tbl spoon of chopped Green onion
    • 1 bunch Thai basil
    • 1 Lime cut into 4 wedges
    • Hoisin sauce
    • Sriracha

Steps to Make Pho

  1. Roast bones and onions

    Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F. Place the bones on a baking sheet and roast in the oven until all are brown (about 1 hour). Place both halves of the cut onion on baking sheet and roast onion another rak until blacked and soft (about 45 minutes).

  2. Make the broth

    Combine the roasted beef bones and onions, ginger, salt, star anise, fish sauce, and water in a large stock pot. Bring to a boil then turn to low heatallow it to simmer for roughly 6 to 10 hours.

  3. Soak the rice noodles

    About 1.5 hours prior to the end of simmering the brother, place the dry rice noodles into a container filled with room temperature water. Let it soak for 1 hour.

  4. Cook the rice noodles

    First, fill a small pot with water and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, cook the rice noodles in the boiling water until the noodles are soft but not too soft to the point they are mushy. Check the texture every 30 seconds to your liking. Drain the cooked noodles and place them in a bowl.

  5. Plate it!

    You're on the last stretch! Finally, garnish the bowl of cooked noodles with thinnly cut sirloin, cilantro, green onions, squeezed lime, and other toppings you might want. Once finished, pour some of that yummy broth you had simmering into your bowl. The meat will be cooked by the hot soup! If the meat is too pink for you, you can cook it to your liking in the broth itself.

Congrats! You've made Pho!

Enjoy your light and delicious cuisine. You'll realize how homey it will make you feel and can cook this whenever you're down in the dumps!
